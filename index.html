DAY 1 — DIVISION SEEDING SCENARIOS
DIVISION SCENARIOS
Two-Team Tiebreaker
TB 1
Winner of the Preliminary Round game between the two tied teams gains the higher position.
TB 2
The team with the most wins in the Preliminary Round gains the higher position.
TB 3
Best Goal Average using all Preliminary Round games: GF ÷ (GF + GA). Higher percentage advances. Example: 10 GF, 4 GA → 10 ÷ 14 = .714
TB 4
Fewest penalty minutes in all Preliminary Round games.
TB 5
The team that scored the first goal in the head-to-head game between the tied teams.
TB 6
Single coin toss determines the higher position.
Three or More Teams Tied
TB 1
Most wins in head-to-head games among tied teams only.
TB 2
Best Goal Average in head-to-head games among tied teams only: GF ÷ (GF + GA). Once a team is seeded, remaining tied teams continue — do not revert to 2-team tiebreaker.
TB 3
Fewest goals against in all Preliminary Round games played.
TB 4
Most goals for in all Preliminary Round games played.
TB 5
Fewest penalty minutes in all Preliminary Round games.
TB 6
Coin toss. In a 3-team coin toss, the odd team gains the highest position.
Key Notes
⚠
Points always come first. A team with more points ranks higher regardless of tiebreakers.
⚠
Day 1 boundary (4th/5th): If teams are tied on the A/B Division cutoff, tiebreakers above determine which division they enter.
⚠
GA% formula: GF ÷ (GF + GA) — not GF ÷ GA. Goals matter even in blowout wins — protect your percentage.

📋 Google Sheet Template Structure

Your Google Sheet needs 3 tabs named exactly as shown. You only edit the white/yellow cells — the rest is calculated.
TAB 1: "Day1" — Columns A through D
A: Game B: Visitor Goals C: Home Goals D: Notes
TAB 2: "DivA" — Same structure for A Division round-robin (6 games)
A: Game B: Visitor Goals C: Home Goals D: Notes
TAB 3: "DivB" — Same structure for B Division round-robin (6 games)
Same layout as DivA — 6 rows for the 6 B Division round-robin games.
💡 Leave cells blank for unplayed games — the app treats blank as "not yet played".
💡 Enter whole numbers only (0, 1, 2, 3…) — no decimals.
